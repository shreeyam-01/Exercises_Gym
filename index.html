EXERCISES

CHEST

Perform the flat bench press using an 18-20 kg barbell. Lie on a sturdy bench, feet flat. Targeting chest muscles, grip the bar slightly wider than shoulders. Prioritize safety by having a spotter, securing weights, and maintaining proper form to prevent strain and ensure a controlled descent and ascent.

FLAT BENCH PRESS


Execute the incline bench press using an 18-20 kg barbell. Position on an inclined bench with a 15-30 degree angle. Targeting the upper chest, grasp the bar wider than shoulders. Prioritize safety with a spotter, secure weights, and maintain controlled movements for optimal effectiveness and injury prevention.

INCLINE BENCH PRESS


Perform the decline bench press using an 18-20 kg barbell. Lie on a declined bench with a 15-30 degree angle. This targets the lower chest. Grip the bar wider than shoulders. Ensure safety with a spotter, secure weights, and maintain controlled movements to optimize effectiveness and prevent injuries.

DECLINE BENCH PRESS


BACK

Utilize a cable machine for lat pulldowns. Attach a wide grip bar. This exercise targets the latissimus dorsi muscles, enhancing upper back strength. Ensure a stable seated position, a controlled pace, and proper form. Start with a weight manageable for your fitness level, gradually increasing for progression while prioritizing safety.

LAT PULLDOWNS


Perform cable rows using a cable machine with a V-bar attachment. Targeting the upper back muscles, sit upright, extend arms, and pull the cable towards your torso. Maintain a neutral spine and controlled movements. Adjust the weight appropriately, prioritize proper form, and engage core muscles for stability, ensuring a safe and effective workout.

CABLE ROWS


Conduct 45-degree hyperextensions using a hyperextension bench. Targeting the lower back, lie facedown with hips on the pad, and upper body hanging off. Engage the lower back muscles to lift the upper body parallel to the ground. Ensure proper alignment, control, and start with a manageable range of motion, gradually progressing. Prioritize safety by maintaining a neutral spine throughout the exercise.

LEG

Perform squats with an 18-20 kg barbell. This compound exercise targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and core. Ensure proper form by keeping your back straight, knees aligned with toes, and lowering your hips until thighs are parallel to the ground. Use a spotter for safety, start with manageable weights, and progressively increase.

SQUATS


Execute Romanian deadlifts with an 18-20 kg barbell. This targets the hamstrings, glutes, and lower back. Maintain a straight back, hinge at the hips, and lower the bar towards the ground. Ensure safety with a proper warm-up, controlled movements, and avoid rounding the back. Use proper form and start with lighter weights before progressing.

ROMANIAN DEADLIFT


Perform calf raises with or without equipment; use a calf raise machine or hold dumbbells. Targeting the calf muscles, rise onto your toes and lower back down. Ensure a stable surface, start with manageable weights, and maintain controlled movements. Avoid locking your knees, prioritize proper form, and progress gradually for safety and effectiveness.
